Our Purpose

Encouraging gifts that strengthen our mission over time

The St. Peter Foundation was formed in cooperation with St. Peter Evangelical Lutheran Church of Gilberts, LCMS. Our purpose is to encourage and develop gifts that strengthen the mission of St. Peter Lutheran Church and help accomplish that mission over time.

We hold the promise to be biblically sound, to enrich our community, and to meet people’s needs through all seasons of life — here, there, and everywhere, in partnership with the Lutheran Church—Missouri Synod, Northern Illinois District.

A corporate steward

Foundation funds advance the mission of the congregation with an emphasis on ministry beyond the limitations normally associated with the annual operating budget — making possible what the yearly offering alone cannot.

Gifts on your terms

As individual stewards, members may designate their gifts — directing the use of the substance or the earnings of the fund — so long as the gift is acceptable under the Foundation’s Gift Acceptance Policy.

Faithfully governed

An Illinois not-for-profit corporation, the Foundation complies with all applicable laws, ensures accurate use of designated gifts, and is overseen by a volunteer Board of Directors drawn from our congregation.

Why give to the Foundation?

A gift that gives again, year after year

Giving to the church and giving to the Foundation work hand in hand. Both honor God — they simply serve different seasons of the church’s life.

Giving to the Church

The harvest of a single season

Your weekly offering funds today’s ministry — worship, staff, programs, and the day-to-day work of the congregation. It is essential, and it is spent within the year it is given.

Meets the needs of this year’s budget.
Giving to the Foundation

The field that yields every year

Your gift is invested and preserved. The principal endures, and a portion of the earnings is distributed each year — so a single gift continues to support ministry for generations to come.

What Your Gift Supports

Helping grow the missions and ministries of St. Peter

The Foundation connects faithful stewards to the work closest to their hearts — honoring God in support of the mission and ministries of the church.

Church Endowment

Provides for the future preservation and expansion of St. Peter Lutheran Church and helps assure funds for ministry in the years ahead.

Human Care

Meets people’s needs through all seasons of life — caring for our neighbors in the congregation and the wider community.

Missions & Outreach

Supports the community and global outreach ministries of St. Peter, sharing the Gospel here, there, and everywhere.

Continuing Education

Encourages and assists members and church workers pursuing Lutheran education and formation for a lifetime of service.

Capital Improvements

Maintains our buildings, structures, and grounds — providing safety and welcome for all who use our facilities.

General Operating

Strengthens the overall financial stability of the church, now and well into the future, as needs arise.

Gifts may be designated to a specific purpose, or given undesignated for the Board to direct where the need is greatest. The Foundation’s funding priorities are reviewed by the Board from time to time.

Ways to Give

Many paths to a generous gift

The Foundation can receive a wide range of gifts. Whatever form your generosity takes, every donor receives a personalized acknowledgement within ten business days.

Outright Gifts
  • Cash & checks
  • Stocks, bonds & securities
  • Items of property
  • Real estate
Planned & Legacy Gifts
  • Bequests through your will or trust
  • Retirement plan & IRA gifts
  • Life insurance & annuities
  • Charitable remainder & lead trusts
Funds & Endowments
  • Gifts to the Church Endowment
  • Donor-advised fund gifts
  • Establishing a named endowment
  • New endowments require a $25,000 minimum and Board approval.
How to Make a Gift

Give by check today

Make your check payable to the St. Peter Foundation, with a note indicating the fund or purpose you wish to support. Gifts may be placed in the offering or mailed to the church office, directed to the Foundation.

For gifts of stock, retirement assets, real estate, or a gift through your estate, please contact the Foundation so we can help you give in the way that serves you best.
