Gift Acceptance Policy

This policy gives direction to the Foundation’s receipt of gifts so that we remain a faithful servant to both our donors and their gifts — providing a consistent framework for gifts of cash or non-cash property for annual, capital, or endowed purposes.

Adopted by
St. Peter Foundation Board of Directors
Status
Current policy · under board review (2026)

I

Guiding Principles

These policies follow a pattern intended to help fund, protect, and project the mission of the Foundation and its ministries, both now and in the future. The Foundation will comply with all federal, state, and municipal laws.

The Foundation Board ensures the accurate use of designated gifts, optimal management of all funds, and truthful reporting. An annual audit will be performed by a certified auditing firm approved by the Foundation Board.

It is essential that the Foundation’s planned-giving program take a low-key, informational approach — one that creates active interest among the members who respond, rather than pressure.

III

How Gifts Are Designated

Undesignated gifts

Gifts received without designation from the donor are generally treated as unrestricted funds, applied at the discretion of the Foundation Board to the areas of greatest need and opportunity for ministry.

Donor-designated & restricted gifts

The Foundation considers each donor’s intended designation individually, relative to its practicality and the current needs of the Foundation. As needed, a Foundation representative will meet with the donor to discuss applicability and appropriate alternative designations.

IV

The Church Endowment Fund

The purpose of the Church Endowment Fund is to provide for the future preservation and expansion of St. Peter Lutheran Church, and to have funds available to assist in funding future operating costs.

  • ADonations to this fund should be made payable to the St. Peter Foundation, with a notation directing the gift to the “Church Endowment Fund.”
  • BA personalized receipt and acknowledgement will be delivered to each donor, including a note from the Foundation Board Chairman.
  • CNo funds will be expended for five (5) years. Beginning the sixth year from the date the endowment is established, the Board may distribute a minimum of 5% of the fund’s balance annually, under disbursement guidelines approved by the Board.
  • DInvestment of these endowment funds will be in accordance with the investment guidelines of the Foundation.
V

Establishing a New Endowment

$25,000
minimum initial investment

A minimum initial investment of $25,000 and approval by two-thirds of the Foundation Board membership are required to form a new endowment. The donor shall provide a statement of purpose and disbursement guidelines for Board approval and acceptance.

VI

Receipt & Acknowledgement

All Foundation gifts received are directed to the Foundation Office for entry, then deposited into the Foundation bank account. All donors receive a personalized receipt and acknowledgement within ten (10) business days of the gift.

If an undesignated gift is received, the donor is contacted to determine their wishes for its use. The Foundation Office sends quarterly statements, when volume dictates, and annual statements to all Foundation donors.
